# PAT004

## Summary
- Current active truck model.
- Long 6x4 design.
- Four rear drive motors total, wired in parallel per side.
- Very strong mechanically, but long enough that turn radius may later benefit from real differential drive.

## Dimensions
- Total vehicle width: `135 mm`
- Width measured across the outside of the wheels: `130 mm`
- Total vehicle length: `410 mm`
- Height including antenna: `200 mm`

## Wheels
- Wheel diameter outside tires: `50 mm`
- Wheel width: `10 mm`
- Rear drive wheel center-to-center spacing: `60 mm`

## Longitudinal Geometry
- Center-to-center distance from front steering wheel to the last rear drive wheel: `250 mm`
- Front overhang from front wheel center line: `80 mm`
- Rear overhang from rearmost wheel center line: `80 mm`

## Drive Layout
- 6x4 vehicle.
- Steering is front-servo based.
- Steering servo is driven directly from the controller board, not through the IO expander.
- Current steering calibration endpoints for this truck are controller PWM units, not physical degrees:
  - configured full-left endpoint: `55`
  - configured full-right endpoint: `110`
  - basis: ESP32 LEDC duty counts at `50 Hz` with `10-bit` resolution
- Rear drive uses four motors total.
- Motors are electrically connected in parallel per side.
- Differential left-right drive is architecturally possible, but not currently used to improve turning.

## Electronics
- Has the controller board.
- Has the IO expander board.

## Sensors
- Ultrasonic sensors in all four directions.
- Two front-mounted `VL53L0X` sensors are currently installed.
- Front `VL53L0X` center-to-center spacing: `105 mm`
- Front right `VL53L0X`: IO expander port `1`
- Front left `VL53L0X`: IO expander port `2`

## Lighting
- Lighting exists and is already documented elsewhere in the project context.
- Use the IO expander and light-service notes as the source of truth for light-output mapping.

## Notes
- This model is physically long compared with the smaller truck variants.
- Its physical strength and long wheelbase should be considered when later tuning steering, map clearance margins, and any future differential-drive behavior.
- The corrected longitudinal sum is consistent:
  - `80 mm` front overhang
  - `250 mm` front-wheel to last-rear-wheel center distance
  - `80 mm` rear overhang
  - total `410 mm`
